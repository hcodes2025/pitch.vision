A Journey Into Intelligence

(Artificial, General, and Otherwise)

Building Personalized, Safe, Adaptive, and Energy-Efficient AI

Hemant Shukla



Based on firsthand experience, I’ve found that emerging technologies - from GPUs and exascale systems to quantum computing - hold little value without practical, accessible applications. This principle is especially true for AI today. What ultimately gives these technologies meaning is not their theoretical potential but how they empower people through well-crafted and accessible tools.

My interest lies in building adaptive, always-learning personal AI.

This vision includes AI systems that live on wearables, run locally, and evolve with their user - embodying a quiet intelligence that is always on, always learning, and deeply aligned.

The next evolution of AI must be personalized, adaptive, energy-efficient, and safe. While transformer-based large language models (LLMs) have shown remarkable potential, they also expose significant challenges, particularly in computational power cost, reliability, and safety. These limitations demand rethinking beyond scaling.

I design adaptive, efficient, and aligned systems - whether they run on transformers, spiking neurons, hyperdimensional vectors, or technologies yet to be imagined.

My work focuses on building proof-of-concept applications that explore these hybrid possibilities across verticals. I aim to demonstrate practical paths toward safer and more adaptive AI.

These projects are stepping stones towards the goal that reflects my commitment to building intelligent systems that are personal and accessible. I’m eager to bring this piece to teams shaping the future of AI - through collaboration, partnership, or leadership.



All work presented here is original and ongoing. The code will be released selectively after publication. Please feel free to contact me. I welcome discussion and collaboration. I also ask ideas be credited respectfully.
